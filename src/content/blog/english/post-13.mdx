Cloud cost optimization works best as a recurring operating habit, not a one-time cleanup. Teams need a simple review process that connects infrastructure spend with product usage, release plans, and ownership.

Start by grouping spend around services that the business understands. When each cost area has a clear owner, teams can discuss tradeoffs with more context and less guesswork.

> Cost reviews become useful when they explain what changed, why it changed, and who can act on it.

The review should include usage trends, idle resources, reserved capacity, storage growth, and upcoming product changes. A short weekly review is usually more useful than a long quarterly audit.

## Keep the Process Actionable [.h5]

Every review should end with clear next steps. Assign each action to an owner, record the expected impact, and revisit the result in the next review cycle.

Small recurring improvements compound quickly when teams can see the connection between technical decisions and operating cost.
